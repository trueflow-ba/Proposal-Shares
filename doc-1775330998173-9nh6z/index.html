Section 2: Technical Architecture for Brady's Fish House

The Brady's Fish House AI Receptionist solution is built on a three-layer architecture: the telephony layer (Clarity VoIP), the AI conversation layer (the voice intelligence engine that handles the call), and the integration layer (the middleware that connects call outputs to your ordering system). Each layer is purpose-configured for Brady's Fish House's specific menu, business hours, and ordering workflow. The architecture is designed for reliability and simplicity — there are no fragile custom-coded pieces that require ongoing developer maintenance, and the system is built to handle simultaneous calls without performance degradation.

Clarity VoIP — Telephony & Call Routing Layer: Brady's Fish House's existing Clarity VoIP account serves as the call ingestion point. Inbound calls to your restaurant's main number are routed via configured forwarding rules to the AI Receptionist endpoint. Call routing logic includes business hours scheduling, overflow handling, and emergency bypass rules so that staff can always regain manual control if needed.
AI Voice Engine — Conversational Intelligence Layer: The AI conversation engine processes speech in real time, understands natural language order requests, references the Brady's Fish House menu database, and manages the full order dialogue. The voice model is configured with a persona and vocabulary tuned for a casual, friendly seafood restaurant environment — not a sterile corporate phone system. All dialogue logic, menu data, and order rules are stored and maintained in this layer.
Order Integration Middleware — Data Pipeline Layer: A lightweight integration middleware receives the structured order data output by the AI upon call completion and formats it for injection into Brady's Fish House's ordering system. This middleware also handles call logging, confirmation message generation, and reporting data aggregation. It operates in real time with sub-second latency between call completion and order ticket creation.

Pre-Launch Testing Environment

Before the Brady's Fish House AI Receptionist goes live, we operate a full sandbox environment that mirrors your production setup exactly. Test calls are placed using Clarity VoIP's test routing capabilities, and our team walks through dozens of order scenarios including full orders, partial orders, modifications, edge-case menu questions, and escalation triggers. Brad Smith and his team are invited to participate in the User Acceptance Testing phase to call the sandbox number themselves and experience the system firsthand, providing feedback before any real customer call is handled.
